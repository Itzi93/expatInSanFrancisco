Transport
in San Francisco

San Francisco is a city with an extensive public transport network. Below we show you what are the public transport methods you will find in San Francisco.

Cable Car

Cable Cars tours start at Fisherman's Wharf and go through downtown San Francisco; We recommend that you choose to get up at the stops in the middle to avoid the long queues that are generated in the port.
There is also a network of municipal trams (MUNI), which makes trips within the city. It includes cars of the famous cable car type and a great variety of routes at a very low price. The ticket costs $ 1.5 on regular cars and $ 5 on cable cars.

BART

It is a tram system that connects various points of the Peninsula of San Francisco with cities located to the east.

Bus

There is a wide line of buses that carry out regional and interstate journeys. It is the cheapest and most efficient means of transportation in San Francisco. Greyhound is one of the most important bus lines in San Francisco and travels throughout the Bay, connecting San Francisco with other cities in the State of California and surrounding areas.
Transit Golden Gate, on the other hand, is a company that makes short distance trips. Connect downtown San Francisco with Marin and Sonoma.
Within San Francisco is the SamTrans line that connects the different neighborhoods of the city at an affordable price ($ 1.50 costs each trip).
Finally, the Golden Gate Transit bus service leaves San Francisco, crosses the Golden Gate Bridge and costs $ 3.15. It also has routes to the north that reach Santa Rosa.

Ferry

The Golden Gate Ferry makes trips from the San Francisco Ferry Building to Larkspur and Sausalito (the ticket costs $ 7.10). From Fisherman's Wharf you can also leave at the company that is called "The Blue and Gold Fleet"; it owns boats that go to Oakland and Alameda (the ticket costs $ 5.50), to Angel Island ($ 7.25), to Sausalito, Larkspur and Tiburón (costs about $ 9.00), to Vallejo (costs $ 12.50).

Caltrain

To move from San Francisco to San Jose, or one of the cities that are on this route, it is best to opt for the Caltrain.It has a good value for money: the most expensive ticket costs around $ 4.

Uber & Lyft

Uber and Lyft are one of the best methods to move around the city. They are like taxis, much cheaper and also with the option to share it on your route to make it much cheaper.Many times it is one of the best options to save time, since the distances are very long.Also, if you invite contacts, they give you money to travel for free.
